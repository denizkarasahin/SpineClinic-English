Model Methodology

▸
This section explains how the model works, which assumptions are used, and why these approaches were chosen. It establishes common ground for a productive dialogue with investors.
Year 1 — Real Data
Year 1 revenue and cost figures are calculated in real time from the model's brace ramp, product mix table, and fixed cost sliders. No assumptions are used — it is the direct output of the slider values.
Year-End Mix Baseline
For Year 2-3 unit revenue calculations, the Month 10-12 average is used rather than the annual average. Rationale: the product mix shifts toward premium products over time (sensor, perforated); if the mature mix is not used as the baseline, the projection will be understated.
Istanbul Year 2-5 Cost — Capacity-Derived
For each year: Net = Gross brace revenue − Operating cost. Gross revenue is target brace count × year-end unit net revenue. Istanbul's Year 2-5 operating cost is no longer a flat multiple of Year 1 — it is built up from the time-and-motion capacity engine (see below):

• Structural base (rent, expert orthotist, operator, interns, utilities, kitchen, admin, advertising) inflates off Year 1 at ×1.15 / ×1.18 / ×1.22 / ×1.25 for Years 2-5 — rent-and-salary inflation only.
• Fitting orthotists & workshop support are added on top, sized each year by the capacity engine from that year's monthly brace volume (annual ramp ÷ 12).
• Printers are bought as the ramp crosses each printer's monthly capacity — the incremental capex lands in the year acquired (zeroed if Osteoid Inc. supplies the equipment).
• Branch fitting offices open when required rooms exceed the per-site maximum; their one-time fit-out lands in the opening year, with rent + utilities recurring thereafter.
Time-and-Motion Capacity Engine
Every clinic's physical footprint and staffing are derived from a single time-and-motion model rather than fixed headcount thresholds. From a month's brace volume it computes fitting rooms, fitting orthotists, workshop support staff, printers and (where rooms overflow) branch offices.

• Weekend-peak sizing: the patients are school children, so a large share (default 60%) can only attend on weekends and school holidays. Rooms and fitting staff are therefore sized to the busiest design day — the peak weekend/holiday day — not the smoother weekly average, so the clinic can actually absorb the concentrated demand.
• B2B asymmetry: B2B (wholesale / orthosis-store) braces consume printers only — they never enter a fitting room, take orthotist time, or add workshop hours, since the patient is never seen at the clinic. So B2B scales printers but adds zero rooms, orthotists or support staff.
• Interns and the junior/graduate orthotist remain threshold-gated (a training pipeline, not capacity math); the fitting orthotists and workshop support staff above are the capacity-driven lines.
• B2B stream (Years 2-5): the wholesale / orthosis-store channel ramps its monthly rate from the Year-1 exit rate toward a Year-5 target (b2bHedefAdetYil, default 1,200/yr) over b2bRampYears (default 3), then holds; set the target to 0 to remove it. Revenue = ramped volume × the Year-1 B2B channel's year-end unit net (same doctor-fee / print-cost / royalty deductions as B2C). It flows into consolidated gross, FCF, Year-5 EBITDA and Exit Value as its own visible "Center 1 (B2B channel)" line. B2B lifts printer capex (shared hardware, sized on B2C + B2B volume) but adds no rooms, orthotist or workshop cost.
Izmir / Ankara Revenue Calculation
New center net revenue = Gross revenue − Year 1 operating cost

• Gross revenue: ramp total braces × year-end unit net revenue
• Cost: Istanbul Year 1 cost (or city sliders if custom costs selected)
• Staff: automatically scales according to threshold system
• Advertising: central cost — not charged separately to new centers
Metric definitions
One set of definitions feeds every valuation figure on every page (Summary Exit box, Investor DCF, scenario table) — no figure borrows another's name:

• Operating profit = revenue − all cash operating costs; equipment purchases are expensed when incurred.
• EBITDA = operating profit + that year's expensed equipment/setup added back; no depreciation is modeled because capex is expensed.
• FCF = operating profit − corporate tax at 25% (with loss carryforward).
• Exit Value = Year-5 EBITDA × the exit-multiple slider; exit-year taxation is not modeled (a stated simplification — see shareholder Exit Tax Structuring on the Investor page).

• VAT: orthosis-prosthesis supply is fully exempt (0%) under VAT Law 17/4-s.
• Corporate tax: clinic activity subject to 25% (TGB exemption applies only to Osteoid Inc.).
Royalty & Cutting Mechanism
For each brace, a royalty of €75 × EUR/TRY rate flows to Osteoid Inc. Additionally, for SKUs with perforations (Perforated and Sensor+Perforated), a €— cutting fee is paid to Osteoid Inc. per brace. Both amounts are deducted as expenses in the clinic revenue calculation.

2nd Center Trigger
The Izmir or Ankara decision is tied not to a fixed schedule but to a data-driven trigger: when Istanbul's 4-month moving average monthly net profit turns positive. Financial discipline principle — no scaling before the POC is proven.
