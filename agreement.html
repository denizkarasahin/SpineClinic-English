Investor Agreement — Two-Stage Committed Structure
Structure — Stage 1 & Stage 2
A committed two-closing deal, not a single round: one Shareholders' Agreement and one License Purchase Agreement, both executed at Stage-1 signing. Stage 1 ("Bridge"), €500,000 — funds incorporation of the clinic A.Ş., the Istanbul build-out, and working capital (~€300,000 + reserve); no rights payment at this stage. Stage 2 ("Full Commitment"), ~€1,500,000 — the rights purchase (IP license €500,000 + city exclusivity €500,000, price fixed at Stage 1 signing) + Izmir & Ankara build-outs + remaining reserve — is a binding obligation that closes automatically once objective Istanbul milestones are met: an operational clinic and first [X] braces delivered by Month [Y]. The investor may walk away from Stage 2 only if those milestones are not met. The IP/design-library license and city exclusivity are sold by Osteoid A.Ş. to the clinic company under the License Purchase Agreement — price fixed and disclosed at Stage 1 signing, arm's-length terms, payment due at Stage 2 closing — perpetual, surviving any change of control of the licensor, and escrow-backed (see Technology Availability & Survival below). Exclusivity is performance-conditional only if Osteoid's ownership in the clinic company falls below 50% (see Exclusivity below); it is otherwise unconditional and is never itself for sale.
License Grant
Sold by Osteoid A.Ş. to the clinic company under a License Purchase Agreement executed at Stage 1 signing — price fixed and disclosed at signing, payment due at Stage 2 closing. Perpetual, territory-wide license covering an unlimited number of centers, with no additional license fees per center. Includes satellite sub-licensing rights, all software updates, and all new SKU/brace design releases for the life of the license.
Exclusivity
Exclusivity is unconditional for as long as Osteoid A.Ş. holds 50% or more of the clinic company. If Osteoid's stake falls below 50%, minimum-performance conditions activate — a minimum of [N] braces/year across the network. After [2] consecutive years of failing that minimum, exclusivity reverts to a non-exclusive license; the license itself always survives and is never terminated.
Technology Availability & Survival
Design-library and source materials are held in escrow, with defined service levels for uptime and support. The license and exclusivity survive any change of control of the licensor (Osteoid A.Ş. or its parent) — a change of control cannot be used to terminate or renegotiate either.
Exit Mechanics (Symmetric)
Mutual right of first refusal (ROFR) applies to any share transfer by either party. Tag-along rights protect whichever party is the minority holder at the time of a sale. Drag-along rights apply above a floor price of [€ __]. From Year [3] onward, either party may trigger a mutual buy-sell: the offering party names a price per share, and the receiving party must either sell its stake at that price or buy the offeror's stake at the same price.
Dividend Policy
At least [60]% of distributable profit — after setting aside a defined working-capital reserve — is distributed annually, starting from the first cumulative-positive year.
Entity & Tax Treatment
The clinic company is incorporated as an A.Ş. (joint-stock company), with share certificates printed at closing. This starts the 2-year holding-period clock for the Turkish personal capital-gains tax exemption for Turkish tax-resident shareholders — to be confirmed by the company's YMM (certified public accountant).
Related-Party Hygiene
The License Purchase Agreement is executed at Stage-1 signing as a disclosed, pre-agreed related-party contract between the clinic company and Osteoid A.Ş. — a cash sale at a fixed, arm's-length price, disclosed and priced at signing, with payment due at Stage 2 closing. The investor's related-party veto right applies only to future amendments to this agreement or to new related-party dealings entered into after closing — it does not apply to, and cannot be used to unwind, the agreement disclosed and priced at signing.
Doctor Commission Agreement
Since doctors directly referring patients to a center they co-own creates a grey area, a frontman structure will be used. Shares are held under a designated legal/natural person, not in the doctor's name.
Commission Rate & Accrual — Updated from the Market page
25%
The amount is not paid out as direct cash; it accrues as a separate line item in the company's accounts under the doctor's name. The commission is not contractually binding; it is fulfilled upon a usage request.
Use via Expenditure
The accrued commission is covered directly by the company upon request for professional expenses accepted by the medical chamber, such as conference registration, accommodation, books, and publications. Proof of expenditure is required.
Cash Request & Invoicing
If a cash payment is requested, the doctor or a recommended third party must issue an invoice to the company. The invoice must include VAT; payment will be made within 15 business days from the invoice date.
Channel Share — Equity Conversion Right
Doctors who establish and sustain the referral channel have the right to use their accrued channel shares, in exchange for that contribution, to purchase shares in the company at a discounted price at a later stage. Conversion terms (price discount, duration, minimum accrued share amount) shall be determined by separate mutual agreement between the parties.
Agreement Duration
12 months; automatic renewal provided both parties give 30 days' written notice. Terms may be renegotiated at the end of the first year.
Confidentiality
Patient data is protected under PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law). Technology infrastructure, pricing, and business processes shall remain confidential during and after the term of the agreement.
Termination
Immediate termination for cause (ethical violation, PDPL violation). Otherwise, 30 days' written notice; pending commission payments shall be settled.
Non-Compete Clause
During the term of the agreement, active roles in competing clinical entities in the spinal orthosis field are prohibited. The non-compete clause becomes void upon termination of the agreement.
