How these numbers are calculated
MOIC = Your payout ÷ your ticket. IRR = annualized return over 5 years (simple: MOIC^(1/5)−1). Enterprise Value = Year 5 EBITDA × exit multiple. Your payout = EV × your equity stake. Equity stake = your ticket ÷ post-money valuation. The "For 3× MOIC" row shows the EV and exit multiple required to triple your investment — use it as a target benchmark. Color guide: green = IRR ≥ 25% / MOIC ≥ 3×, yellow = IRR ≥ 15% / MOIC ≥ 2×, red = below.
MOIC and IRR above are for the Lead Investor's own Investor Ticket and Investor Stake — the priced round from The Deal at a Glance (Investor Ticket ÷ Post-Money), not the Contribution Register. Doctor-investors have their own cash amount and stake computed the same way. Physician partners referring patients also earn Sci/Edu/Library fees during Years 1–5 (independent of equity) — this is additional return not captured here.
Return on cash invested is the external investor's cash-on-cash view — capital that actually leaves an investor's pocket (clinic setup cost + working capital, read live from the Investment Breakdown table in Deal Structure & Assumptions). Return on total capital additionally counts Osteoid A.Ş.'s remaining in-kind contribution (machinery, only when Osteoid-owned) as invested capital, so it's always ≤ the cash-only return — IP license and city exclusivity are no longer in-kind here, since the clinic now buys them from Osteoid for cash (the Investor Ticket). Both rows use the same after-tax free cash flow stream as the DCF table, and both react live to the equipment-ownership toggle, the corporate tax toggle, and any P&L slider that moves the Year-1 cash trough. Gross Multiple = cumulative FCF ÷ capital invested, before shareholder-level exit tax. Net-to-Investor Multiple applies the Exit Tax Structuring result (Deal Structure & Assumptions below) to the gain portion only — return of capital itself is never taxed.

Network Structure — Flagship & Satellites
Osteoid scales hub-and-spoke: Istanbul is the flagship — it runs its own clinic and acts as the network's management company. Izmir, Ankara, and future cities are satellites: separate companies, majority-owned by the flagship, each with its own local investors and its own regulatory license. Satellites pay the flagship a management fee on gross revenue, and the flagship holds an equity stake in each satellite's remaining profit — but satellite expansion is funded by local investors at the satellite level, so the flagship's own cap table never dilutes as the network grows. Terminal value uses an exit-multiple method: TV = FCF₅ × Exit Multiple — computed on the same after-tax FCF₅ as the interim cash flows (a proxy for an after-tax earnings multiple, not a pre-tax number an exit buyer would never actually receive). Modeled Value (DCF) = NPV(FCF₁₋₅) + PV(Terminal Value) — the unadjusted model output. Deal Pre-Money = Modeled Value × (1 − Negotiation Discount) — see The Deal at a Glance; the discount is the negotiated concession for single-site execution risk, not a re-run of the DCF itself. No asset floor: IP license and city exclusivity are now a cash sale to the clinic (the Investor Ticket), not an in-kind equity contribution, so there's no contributed-asset value to floor pre-money against. Post-Money = Deal Pre-Money + actual new cash raised (Investor Ticket + any Doctor-Investor cash). Investor Ticket = IP license + city exclusivity (bought from Osteoid A.Ş.) + Working Capital Buffer + Setup cost for Centers 1-3 (Istanbul + Izmir + Ankara, each its own modeled capex plus a manual Overhead adjustment). Investor Stake = Investor Ticket ÷ Post-Money; Doctor-Investor Stake = their cash ÷ Post-Money; Sweat = the vested % up to its cap; Osteoid Stake = the remainder — a priced round, not the Ownership Register below (that Contribution Register is now reference-only, showing machinery in-kind vs cash under the old weighted model — see captable.html). Discount rate (r) is set by the Risk Profile control on The Deal at a Glance, not a generic early-stage-startup premium — see "Why This Venture Is De-Risked" above.

Corporate tax: 25% on profit from clinic operations; no sales exemption (Technology Development Zone exemption belongs solely to Osteoid Inc.). EV is always calculated on EBITDA — the figures above are total enterprise value, not equity value. Your equity value = EV × your ownership percentage.
